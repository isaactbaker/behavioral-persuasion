# idea-002: Skills Development Agent (Social Intelligence Platform)

## Problem
Sales teams learn product knowledge obsessively but never practice the human skills that close deals: reading a room, adapting communication style, managing objections with psychology. Gong tells them WHAT happened on a call — nobody tells them HOW to be more persuasive next time.

## Target Customer
Sales enablement directors and first-line sales managers at mid-market companies ($50M-$500M revenue). Teams where deal size is $10K-$100K and negotiation skill directly impacts quota attainment.

## Solution Concept
An AI agent that integrates with Gong/Chorus to analyze sales calls through a persuasion science lens:
1. **Post-call analysis** — identifies which Cialdini principles the rep used effectively vs. missed opportunities
2. **Pre-call playbook** — generates a tailored persuasion strategy for each contact based on past interactions
3. **Daily micro-practice** — 5-minute persuasion scenarios via Slack/Teams
4. **Team benchmarking** — tracks persuasion skill growth across the team over time
5. **Manager dashboard** — shows which reps need coaching on which persuasion dimensions

## Validation Hypothesis
"If I show a Sales Enablement Director a landing page at $249/seat/mo with Gong integration, 3% will put down a deposit for early access."

## Price Point
$249/seat/mo (team tier). Individual tier $99/mo for solo reps and founders.

## Competition
- Gong ($700-2,000/seat/yr) — conversation intelligence, not training
- Outreach ($100-150/seat/mo) — sequence management
- Salesloft ($100-150/seat/mo) — engagement platform
- Allego ($20-50/seat/mo) — video coaching
- Seismic (enterprise) — content delivery

None apply actual persuasion science. All treat sales as process compliance, not skill development.

## Risk Level
Medium-High — requires Gong/Chorus integration to deliver full value. MVP can start with manual transcript upload, automate integration after first paying customer.

## Key Differentiator
First product that applies evidence-based persuasion science (Cialdini, cognitive biases, framing effects) to sales call analysis. Reps don't just learn "what happened" — they learn "how to be more persuasive."
